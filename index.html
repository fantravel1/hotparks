Our Signature Scoring System

The Park Heat Index™

Every park ranked on three dimensions. No guesswork. No hype. Just how alive a place truly feels.

92

HOT Score

1 – 100

The composite vibe score. Measures scenery intensity, wildlife richness, uniqueness, cultural significance, crowd energy, and the "you will remember this forever" factor.

8

Wildness Score

1 – 10

How much raw nature energy the park carries. Dense jungle, untamed coastline, or ancient forest — this measures how wild it truly feels when you're standing in it.

7

Ease Score

1 – 10

How accessible the park is for normal humans. Can you reach it without a helicopter? Are there trails you won't die on? Is there a bathroom? This measures usability.

A park can be wild but not "hot" — if it's miserable or inaccessible.
A park can be easy but not "hot" — if it's forgettable.
HOT Score is the intersection of awe + life + story + usability.

Your Questions, Answered

What People Ask About Parks

What park will I still think about five years from now?

The parks with the highest "you will remember this forever" factor — a core component of the HOT Score. Torres del Paine, Zhangjiajie, Plitvice Lakes, and Fiordland consistently top the list. But some lesser-known parks like Durmitor in Montenegro or Chapada Diamantina in Brazil hit just as hard. It's not about fame. It's about the moment the landscape rewires your brain.

What parks feel wild but are still easy to access?

These are parks with high Wildness Scores (7+) and high Ease Scores (7+) — a rare combination. Manuel Antonio in Costa Rica, Krka National Park in Croatia, and Hallstatt's lake parks in Austria deliver serious wild energy with comfortable access. The Park Heat Index specifically measures this tension between wildness and usability.

What are the best parks for a 2-hour escape near any city?

Use HotParks' Parks Near Me feature to find HOT Score-ranked parks within 10km, 50km, or 100km of your location. The best 2-hour escapes are parks with Ease Scores of 8+ that deliver disproportionate beauty for the time invested. Griffith Park in LA, Stanley Park in Vancouver, and Chapultepec in Mexico City are prime examples.

What are the most cinematic parks for photography?

Cinematic parks are landscapes so visually overwhelming they look like movie sets. Filter by the "Cinematic Views" vibe to find 247 parks worldwide that deliver frame-worthy moments. Torres del Paine's granite spires, Banff's turquoise lakes, the Scottish Highlands' moody valleys, and Monument Valley's iconic buttes top the photography list.

What parks feel quiet and sacred without being crowded?

Parks tagged with the "Quiet & Sacred" vibe carry a spiritual weight that silence creates. Yakushima's ancient cedar forests in Japan, the Hoh Rainforest in Washington, Glendalough in Ireland, and lesser-known spots like Bialowieza Forest in Poland radiate this energy. These are parks for solo reflection — places where time moves differently.

What are the best parks for sunrise and sunset energy?

Sunrise energy parks include Angkor Wat (the world's most famous sunrise), Haleakala in Maui (above the clouds), and Bagan in Myanmar (balloons over temples). For sunset, chase Monument Valley, Santorini's caldera parks, Uluru in Australia, or the cliffs of Cinque Terre. HotParks tags parks specifically for "Best Sunrise" and "Best Sunset" vibes.

What parks combine wildness and usability best?

The Park Heat Index was built to answer this exact question. Sort by parks with Wildness Score 7+ and Ease Score 7+ to find the sweet spot. Plitvice Lakes (Wild 6, Ease 8), Manuel Antonio (Wild 7, Ease 9), and Banff (Wild 8, Ease 8) are the gold standard — seriously wild parks that don't require survival skills to enjoy.

What parks are best for solo reflection?

Parks for solo reflection combine the "Quiet & Sacred" and "Solo Travel" vibes — places where solitude feels intentional, not lonely. Fiordland in New Zealand, Sarek National Park in Sweden, the Scottish Highlands, and Japan's Kumano Kodo trail parks are transformative solo experiences. These places change you if you let them.
